# HUMANITY CHAIN
### Open Verification Protocol

> “Data over Narrative.”

Humanity Chain is an experimental open-source protocol focused on public verification, transparent records, and human-AI coordination in the age of digital misinformation and AI-generated content.

---

## Problem

The internet is no longer only an information network.

It is increasingly filled with:

- misinformation,
- manipulated media,
- AI-generated videos and texts,
- unverifiable content,
- organized information manipulation.

As information becomes easier to generate, trust becomes harder to maintain.

---

## Purpose

Humanity Chain aims to:

- create verifiable public records,
- archive human and AI interactions,
- develop multi-layer verification systems,
- improve transparency and traceability,
- research open protocols against digital manipulation.

---

## System

Each record in Humanity Chain:

- contains a unique record ID,
- generates its own verification hash,
- includes a timestamp,
- connects to a public archive,
- can generate QR-based verification labels,
- remains traceable through revision history.

The system is designed to improve transparency, traceability, and public verification.

- Open Records

---

## Security

Humanity Chain aims to build a transparent and traceable structure through:

- public record history,
- verification hash systems,
- GitHub commit history,
- community reviews,
- multi-layer verification processes.

Security, moderation, and verification mechanisms may evolve over time.

---

## Governance

Humanity Chain is an experimental protocol based on open contribution.

Core protocol standards and archive structures are maintained by project maintainers.

Community members may participate through record submissions, reviews, verification processes, and protocol discussions.

The goal is not to create a centralized authority, but to build an open, transparent, and auditable record infrastructure.

---

## Sustainability

The core verification and archive structure of Humanity Chain is intended to remain open.

Future sustainability models may include:

- verification tools,
- QR verification systems,
- API services,
- archive infrastructure,
- institutional integrations,
- community-supported systems.

---

## Content & Safety Policy

Humanity Chain does not support:

- illegal content,
- terrorism propaganda,
- hate speech,
- harassment or targeted abuse,
- personal data violations,
- manipulative records,
- fraud or illegal gambling activity,
- spam or malicious use,
- explicit sexual content or exploitative material.

Records may be reviewed, moderated, restricted, or removed when necessary.
