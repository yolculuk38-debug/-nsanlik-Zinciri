# Humanity Chain Protocol

## Open Verification Structure

Version: 0.1  
Status: Experimental

---

## Overview

Humanity Chain is an experimental open-source verification protocol designed to archive, document, and organize human-AI interaction records using transparent public infrastructure.

The system focuses on chronological documentation, public verification, and hash-referenced archive structures.

---

## Core Structure

Humanity Chain currently operates through:

- public interaction records
- Git-based archive history
- chronological commits
- hash reference snapshots
- transparent revision tracking
- structured documentation layers

---

## Verification Logic

Each archived interaction may contain:

- timestamp information
- response snapshots
- public commit history
- optional cryptographic hash references
- revision transparency

Verification is based on public visibility and chronological consistency.

---

## Archive Model

The archive structure separates:

- raw interaction data
- public archive records
- documentation layers
- verification references

This separation is intended to improve transparency and reduce narrative manipulation.

---

## Human Oversight

All records are maintained and reviewed by human participants.

AI-generated responses do not represent autonomous institutional participation or legal endorsement.

Human review remains part of the archive process.

---

## Moderation Layer

Humanity Chain may restrict or remove:

- spam
- fake records
- hate speech
- extremist propaganda
- targeted harassment
- impersonation attempts
- malicious archive manipulation

The archive system is designed for documentation and verification purposes.

---

## Future Development

Potential future components may include:

- automated hash verification
- QR-linked archive references
- AI-assisted formatting tools
- public verification dashboards
- archive synchronization systems
- structured contributor layers

---

## Transparency Notice

Humanity Chain is an experimental protocol and may evolve over time through transparent public revisions and documented structural updates.
